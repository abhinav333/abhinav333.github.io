    Approximate Computing for MIMO detection

    Place: LSSI Lab, UQTR, QC , Canada

    • Computing applications have surged in quantity and complexity in recent times and hence the demand for advanced computing capabilities in integrated circuits has also been increasing proportionally.
    • Energy efficiency and logic density are becoming critical factors while designing integrated circuits for advanced computing applications.
    • Exploiting the inherent error resiliency of computing applications, novel approximate circuit techniques are being actively explored in the research domain that increases the energy efficiency and decrease the resource consumption of the circuit by penalizing the application accuracy.
    • Arithmetic multiplication operation is a computationally intensive task at the crux of most computing applications, especially in signal processing and communications applications.
    • The proposed work is built on the rationale of approximation techniques for FPGA based signed multipliers, addressing the challenge of the availability of a concise heuristic methodology for inducing controlled approximation in FPGA multiplication circuits to achieve intended level of trade-off between energy efficiency and resource consumption with error performance..

    Improving Accuracy of Superpixel Segmentation using Colour Histogram based Seed Initialization

    Place: HESL Lab, NTU SCSE, Singapore

    • Superpixels are perceptually meaningful segments of an image formed by grouping several image pixels together based on a common property (e.g. colour) and provide high-level abstraction of the raw image which can be exploited for accelerating computer vision applications.
    • In this work, Histogram Super Linear Iterative Clustering (SLIC) algorithm is prototyped to improve the accuracy of SLIC algorithm by using colour histogram to induce colour sensitivity during the seed initialization, where the quantized colour histogram captures the localized colour profiles within uniform grids in the raw image.
    • The quality of the initial seeding can be controlled by user defined parameters such as histogram suppression window and the maximum seeds per grid box.
    • Label enforcement of erroneous pixels is implemented over the superpixel segmented image, which is achieved by asserting a pixel with a particular cluster label based on the majority labels surrounding it.
